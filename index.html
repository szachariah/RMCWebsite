NASA RMC is an annually held competition held at the NASA Kennedy Space Center, Florida where teams from different communities and universities build robots that traverse and mine Martian soil (regolith). NASA’s ultimate objective for this competition is for the further advancement of Space Exploration. This in turn, NASA has given college students the task to find innovative mining techniques to be used for obtaining the water-ice found under the surface of the Martian soil. As a result, this will allow humans to “live off the land” or in scientific terms “In-Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU)”. In the competition, points are awarded based on creative design, amount of regolith mined, autonomous operation of the robot, and innovative ideas.

This year, the students will be attempting a new design to increase mining performance and further implementation of autonomous operation. The current design our team is pursuing is a “bucket ladder mixed with the auger design” (Figure 2, Figure 3). They are both known for aggressive mining, especially when working with gravel like substance (icy regolith). The drive system of the robot is called, “Rocker Bogie” (Figure 4) which is a specific type of drivetrain famously used by the Mars rover, Curiosity. This drivetrain works best especially when driving on uneven terrain like Martian soil. We also plan to use small brushless motors to maintain a lightweight design and give us the extra power to traverse the Martian terrain. In order to power the robot, we plan to use Lithium-Polymer battery packs which allow high current draw rate, which would be ideal for our drive system.
