NASA RMC is an annually held competition held at the NASA Kennedy Space Center, Florida where teams from different communities and universities build robots that traverse and mine Martian soil (regolith). NASA’s ultimate objective for this competition is for the further advancement of Space Exploration. This in turn, NASA has given college students the task to find innovative mining techniques to be used for obtaining the water-ice found under the surface of the Martian soil.

As a result, this will allow humans to “live off the land” or in scientific terms “In-Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU)”. In the competition, points are awarded based on creative design, amount of regolith mined, autonomous operation of the robot, and innovative ideas.
